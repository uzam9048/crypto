Tutorial on Bearish Engulfing Patterns

What is Bearish Engulfing Candlestick Pattern?
Bearish Engulfing Candlestick Pattern is formed generally at the end of a uptrend, or near a potential resistance. Bearish Candlestick pattern is a reliable reversal pattern and since it is formed at the top of a uptrend, it is also known as top reversal or bearish reversal pattern. Basically this pattern is made up of two candlestick or can say it takes 2 days for this pattern to be formed.

Day 1. On day one a small bullish candlestick is formed.
Day 2. On the second day a larger bearish candlestick is formed such that the body of the bearish candlestick completely overshadows or engulf the body of the bullish candlestick formed on Day 1.
